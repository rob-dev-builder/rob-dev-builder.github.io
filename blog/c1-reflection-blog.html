Reflections

What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

So far I think the EDA experience is very unique. It seems like a very bold way to teach web development. It is very diffeent from other web developer courses I have heard about.

What are your impressions?

I am really impressed so far. I really like the idea that the cohort is encrouaged to help each other and learn from each other as much as possible. I also feel a little bit overwhelmed sometimes when I think about the end goal of becoming a web developer. However, I think the prep work and help from fellow students helps to ease that anxiety.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

I really want to contribute to making the learning experience as fun and interesting as possioble. I think that is the best way to engage with the EDA culture.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

I did a lot of research before signing up to EDA. So, I had a pretty good idea of what to expect before I joined - and I like it!

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

Yes! and yes I'm nervious and that makes me more excited!
