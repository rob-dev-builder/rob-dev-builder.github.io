Chapter three - Search inside yourself

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?

Unfortunately I'm by myself at the moment and don't have anyone to test this with. However, I did find it interesting and will put this into practise when I see a friend later today. I think the idea of looping (confirming what was said) and dipping (checking in with yourself) while listening is very useful as these techniques will make you a better listener.

Which of the three suggested techniques will you use to sustain your practice?

I will use the 'Do less than you can' technique. Currently I can concentrate for about five minutes before it starts to feel like a chore. Therefore, I will start aiming for three minutes at more regular intervals.
I will also use the 'One breath a day' technique. I like the idea of intending to mediate is itself a mediation. Therefore, focusing on just one full breath a day is a easy way to maintain the habit of being mindful.

Any other musings?

I like the idea of 'looping' whereby you repeat back to the speaker what you think you heard them say. This is a great way to make sure communication is working. This can be used in all areas of life.
